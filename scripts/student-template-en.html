Virtual Machine Usage Regulations

Effective from: June 20, 2025

1. Purpose and Scope of Regulations

These regulations (hereinafter: "Regulations") define the rules for using virtual machines (hereinafter: "VM" or "Virtual Machines") provided to participants (hereinafter: "Participant" or "User") as part of training organized by Sages sp. z o.o. (hereinafter: "Organizer"). The purpose of the Regulations is to ensure safe, lawful, and ethical use of the infrastructure, as well as to limit the potential legal liability of the Organizer.

2. Definitions

  1. Virtual Machine (VM) – a separate, isolated computing environment provided to the Participant by the Organizer exclusively for the duration and purposes of the training.
  2. System – the entirety of technical infrastructure and software within which Virtual Machines operate.
  3. Training Materials – content and resources provided to Participants by the Organizer as part of the training.
  4. Prohibited Actions – any activities contrary to law, Regulations, good practices, or violating the rights of third parties.

3. Access Conditions

  1. The Organizer grants the Participant access to the VM for the duration of the training and under the conditions specified in the Regulations.
  2. Access credentials are personal and may not be shared with third parties.
  3. Login data is confidential information; the Participant is obliged not to disclose it.

4. Usage Rules

4.1. Permitted Actions

  1. Completing tasks provided for in the training program.
  2. Installing and configuring software necessary to perform exercises, provided it does not violate section 4.2.

4.2. Prohibited Actions

  1. Using VM for any illegal activity, in particular:
    • unauthorized access to computer systems (hacking, phishing, etc.);
    • distributing malicious software;
    • violating copyright or licensing rights (piracy, illegal content sharing, cracks, "warez");
    • storing, processing, or sharing illegal content (e.g., child pornography, hate speech, extremist content);
    • conducting DDoS attacks, port scanning, or other activities interfering with third-party systems.
  2. Modifying System security settings without the Organizer's consent.
  3. Sharing VM resources with other people.
  4. Using VM for commercial or private purposes unrelated to the training.

5. Activity Monitoring and Logging

  1. All activity in the System may be monitored, logged, and analyzed by the Organizer or entities authorized by them.
  2. Logs may be used for security purposes, system development, and when necessary to demonstrate legal compliance.
  3. The Participant consents to this by starting to use the VM.

6. Personal Data Protection and Confidentiality

  1. The Organizer processes Participants' personal data in accordance with applicable regulations (particularly GDPR) only to the extent necessary for training implementation.
  2. The Participant undertakes not to process personal data in the VM that is not necessary for completing exercises.
  3. The User is responsible for the confidentiality of data they enter into the VM.

7. Participant's Liability

  1. The Participant bears full civil, criminal, and administrative liability for their actions in the VM.
  2. The Participant undertakes to release the Organizer from liability for any third-party claims arising from the Participant's Prohibited Actions.

8. Organizer's Liability

  1. The Organizer exercises due diligence in maintaining the System but is not liable for damages resulting from improper or non-compliant use of the VM by the Participant.
  2. The Organizer is not responsible for content generated and stored by Participants in the VM.

9. Violations and Sanctions

  1. In case of violation of the Regulations, the Organizer may, at their discretion:
    • block or restrict access to the VM;
    • exclude the Participant from training without the right to refund;
    • notify appropriate law enforcement agencies or take legal action.
  2. Actions taken in connection with section 9.1 do not exclude the Organizer's right to seek compensation.

10. Security Incident Reporting

The Participant undertakes to immediately inform the Organizer of any security incident, irregularity, or suspicion of violation of the Regulations.

11. Final Provisions

  1. The Regulations come into force on the date specified in the header.
  2. The Organizer reserves the right to introduce changes to the Regulations; changes are effective from the moment of their publication and communication to Participants.
  3. In matters not regulated by the Regulations, the provisions of Polish law apply, in particular the Civil Code and the Act on Electronic Services.
  4. Any disputes arising in connection with the use of VM will be resolved by the court with jurisdiction for the Organizer's registered office, unless mandatory provisions state otherwise.
  5. Starting to use the Virtual Machine is equivalent to accepting the Regulations.
