Regulations for Using Virtual Machines During Training

Version: 1.0 – effective from 1 January 2025

§ 1. General Provisions

  1. These Regulations define the rules for using virtual machines (hereinafter: “VM”) provided by the training instructor (hereinafter: the “Administrator”) to participants of training courses (hereinafter: the “User”).
  2. Each User is required to read these Regulations before starting to use the VM and to accept them through the login system.
  3. If the User does not accept the Regulations, an SSH/RDP connection to the virtual machine cannot be established.

§ 2. Access Rules

  1. Access to the VM is granted solely for the duration of the training and for educational purposes related to the training curriculum.
  2. Each User receives individual access credentials (login such as student1, student2, etc. and a password) sent to the e‑mail address provided.
  3. Access credentials are confidential and may not be shared with third parties.
  4. Access is provided through a proxy server that logs all connections.

§ 3. Permitted Use

The User may use the VM exclusively for:

  • completing tasks and exercises specified in the training programme,
  • installing software necessary to perform the training exercises,
  • testing solutions within the scope of the training topics.

§ 4. Prohibited Activities

  1. Security‑related:
    • conducting any network attacks (DDoS, brute‑force, port scanning),
    • attempting unauthorised access to other systems,
    • installing or distributing malicious software,
    • activities aimed at bypassing system security measures.
  2. Legal compliance:
    • using the VM for any illegal activity,
    • infringing copyrights or licence terms,
    • storing or sharing illegal content,
    • conducting commercial activity.
  3. Ethical conduct:
    • using VM resources for purposes unrelated to the training,
    • actions that may disrupt the work of other Users,
    • modifying system settings without the Administrator’s consent.

§ 5. Monitoring and Logging

  1. The Administrator records:
    • all SSH/RDP connections (date, time, user),
    • acceptance of the Regulations by each User,
    • the source IP address of the connection,
    • basic system activity.
  2. The purposes of logging are:
    • to ensure infrastructure security,
    • to demonstrate Users’ compliance with the rules,
    • to protect against legal claims.

§ 6. Liability

  1. The User is fully liable for:
    • all actions performed using the assigned credentials,
    • damages resulting from breaches of these Regulations,
    • the legal consequences of their actions.
  2. The Administrator is not liable for:
    • the User’s actions that are unlawful or violate the Regulations,
    • loss of data entered by the User,
    • interruptions in access caused by maintenance.
  3. The User undertakes to:
    • hold the Administrator harmless from any liability related to the User’s actions,
    • cover any damages resulting from breaches of the Regulations.

§ 7. Sanctions

If the Regulations are breached, the Administrator may:

  1. immediately block access to the VM,
  2. exclude the User from the training without refund,
  3. notify the relevant law‑enforcement authorities,
  4. seek compensation through legal proceedings.

§ 8. Personal Data Protection

  1. Data controller:

    SAGES Sp. z o.o., ul. Nowogrodzka 62c, 00‑695 Warsaw, Poland
    Contact: iod@sages.pl

  2. Scope of data processed:
    • system login (student1, student2, etc.),
    • e‑mail address (for sending access credentials),
    • connection IP address,
    • date and time of acceptance of the Regulations.
  3. Legal basis:
    • Article 6(1)(f) GDPR – legitimate interest of the controller (infrastructure security),
    • Article 6(1)(b) GDPR – performance of the training agreement.
  4. Retention period:
    • system logs: 90 days after the end of the training,
    • contact data: until consent is withdrawn.
  5. User rights:
    • access to data,
    • rectification or deletion of data,
    • filing a complaint with the Polish Data Protection Authority (PUODO).

§ 9. Final Provisions

  1. Acceptance of the Regulations takes place by entering the appropriate command or confirming them during login to the VM.
  2. The Administrator reserves the right to amend the Regulations; Users will be notified of any changes.
  3. Any matters not regulated by these Regulations are governed by Polish law.
  4. Any disputes shall be settled by the court with jurisdiction over the Administrator’s registered office.
